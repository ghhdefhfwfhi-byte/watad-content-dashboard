Client Overview
In April, FAB Iraq's campaigns reached 455,200 people across Iraq with a spend of $588.74 — a 9% reduction vs March while maintaining strong impression volume at 891K (+10%). The campaigns generated 1,099 leads/conversions and 712 Instagram follows. CPM rose to $1.44 vs $1.18 in March, driven by the Sales campaign, while the Awareness objective continues to outperform market benchmarks at $0.23 CPM on Facebook.

Strategic Insights
01
CPM Efficiency Gap
April CPM rose to $1.44 vs $1.18 in March — a 22% increase driven by the Sales campaign ($323 spend). Awareness placements continue outperforming at $0.23 CPM on Facebook.
02
Lead Volume Decline
Leads dropped 42.8% month-over-month (1,921 → 1,099), correlating with reduced Sales campaign frequency. IG Follows also fell 64% (1,995 → 712).
03
Traffic Quality vs Volume
Traffic Ads delivered 9,670 combined clicks at CPCs under $0.01 — exceptional efficiency. CTR dropped from 6.42% to 2.10%, suggesting new audiences are less pre-qualified.
Next Steps — Recommended Actions
01
Restore Lead Volume
Leads dropped 42.8% MoM. Reallocate 15–20% of Sales budget to lead-gen placements. Retest March ad sets that generated 1,921 conversions vs current "sara 1" creative.
● High Priority
02
Fix CTR Drop (6.42% → 2.10%)
CTR decline signals audience fatigue. Refresh ad creatives on Awareness placements and introduce custom audiences based on March high-engagement users.
● High Priority
03
Scale Nineveh & Basra
Nineveh delivered 49,782 impressions at $24.93 (CPM ~$0.50). Basra similar at $0.73 CPM. Both are under-indexed vs Baghdad and should receive increased budget allocation.
● Medium Priority
